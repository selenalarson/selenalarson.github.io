About

I'm a journalist for The Daily Dot in San Francisco covering the intersection of technology, culture and social justice.

With a unique background in journalism and communications across the health, education, technology and economic development fields, I aim to tell stories that advocate for the public good and represent diverse voices that often go unheard.

Previously, I was the social reporter for ReadWrite, a freelance journalist and multimedia producer working as a host for creativeLIVE, writing for publications including VentureBeat, AZ Tech Beat and SheKnows, and as an editorial professional helping small businesses tell their story online.

My work has appeared in Lifehacker, The Arizona Republic, Business Insider, and others.

I have experience in TV and radio/podcast productions, and I regularly appear on TWiT.TV and radio shows including BBC's World Have Your Say and the Australian Broadcasting Company's Future Tense program.

I've worked for the Downtown Chandler Community Partnership, FOX Sports Arizona, and Banner Health. I cofounded the magazine Gateway to the East Valley with my best friend and mentor Jennifer Lindley, and served as the assistant editor for over a year.

In my spare time, I'm writing a fiction novel, a series on Medium, and occasionally updating my travel blog, Seeking Cities. My award-winning multimedia pieces include Child Slaves of West Africa, Scars of War and Living the American Dream.

I was an anchor at Gangplank, a collaborative workspace in Chandler, Ariz. where I volunteered my time as a public relations consultant for startups and led their student STEM education program, GangplankJr.
